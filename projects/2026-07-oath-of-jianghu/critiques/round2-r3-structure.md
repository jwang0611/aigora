# R3 Structural Analyst — 《三缄》Round 2

## Causal chain map

The story runs on a genuine mechanical engine, not a string of events: **order → falsified documents → detection → confrontation → recalculated shortfall → tiger-lure plan devised with an explicit, acknowledged risk (the wall-base woodpile) → guard assigned to that risk → risk materializes → fire reaches clinic → deaths → confession that omits/reshapes the truth → decades of silence → frame closes.** Every major object introduced pays off: the whistle and its two-code signal (§2 → §4), the triage rule (§2 foreshadow → §4 mechanism of Su Wan's death), the woodpile risk (§3 explicit "we're not sure we can hold this" setup → §4 payoff), Zheng Han's evidence-only, no-leniency ethic (§2 → §5 verdict). Nothing is a dead scene; removing §2's private pact or §3's map scene would break §4's ending line and the bridge death respectively.

**On the fire specifically**: it is planted and largely earned, not a bare convenience — the characters name the exact risk (wind shift onto the woodpile), explicitly doubt their own mitigation ("能不能压得住,谁心里都没底"), and assign a watcher. That's the discipline the rubric rewards ("felt as inevitable, not felt as 'really?'"). But the actual trigger stacks **two** independent strokes of bad luck at once — wind stronger than expected, *and* an unrelated second patrol (dispatched by Zheng as ordinary caution) reassigning the one watcher away — landing simultaneously. Individually each is earned; together they read as double-insurance that the disaster *will* happen, which nudges the turn back toward convenient. Tying the second patrol's dispatch causally to the fire itself (rather than parallel bad luck) would tighten this to a single, inevitable failure point.

Also worth flagging on causal grounds: the original order sets a **3-day** deadline for burning the granary and demolishing the bridge/road; the depicted sequence of discovery (day 4) → confrontation (day 4+3) → planned action (day 4+3+3) lands the climax roughly **10 days** after the order, with no in-text acknowledgment that the deadline was blown by a week — jarring for a commander (Zheng) established as meticulous enough to catch a two-car discrepancy in withdrawal paperwork.

## Knowledge-state audit

- **The secret order & its penalty**: Zheng Han (author, always) → Gu learns at the order-issuing assembly (§2) → Shen references the penalty as known fact shortly after (§2), plausibly via his registrar role even though we don't see him briefed — acceptable off-page/background knowledge.
- **Su Wan's ignorance of the scheme**: explicitly confirmed in dialogue ("她只知道我在想办法多争时间,不知道我是怎么争的") and her behavior in §4 never contradicts this — clean, no violation.
- **The tiger-lure plan and the woodpile risk**: known only to Gu, Shen, and (partially) Zhang Laoshuan — consistent throughout; Zhang's refusal to explain the woodpile's importance to the second patrol is causally motivated by the secrecy of the scheme itself, not authorial convenience. This is actually a strong thematic echo — the same silence-instinct that defines Gu's whole life is what kills Su Wan.
- **Gu's unspoken feelings for Su Wan**: seeded lightly (wiping ash from her face "是这辈子从没在她面前做过的" motion, the shout with unprecedented force) and never over-explained — good restraint, matches the deliberate ambiguity left around Shen's unfinished last words.
- **Violation — POV contamination (§4)**: "苏晚已经在药庐里指挥了。她仍按旧例排序……" is narrated as flat fact while Gu is outside organizing firebreaks and explicitly "没能立刻进去." Everywhere else in the story, Su Wan's interiority is mediated through Gu's direct sensory presence (the window scene in §2, the grocer's-family scene in §2) — here that discipline breaks at the single most important beat, with no hedge ("大约"/"像是") and no later scene establishing that anyone told Gu what happened inside. This is a genuine knowledge-boundary breach per the story's own established rules.
- **Violation — evidentiary logic of the confession (§5)**: the judicial standard is stated explicitly as "不看人在哪里,看批文和粮册上是谁的笔迹、谁的核算" (whose handwriting, whose arithmetic). But §3 establishes the falsified documents were altered by Shen's own hand *before* Gu even knew. Gu's confession claims sole, from-the-start authorship of the tampering. Under the very evidentiary rule the story sets up, this should be checkable and should fail — the text never addresses how Gu's claim survives scrutiny of the actual paper trail.

## Consistency check

- **Whistle object**: fully consistent chain of custody — forged by Shen (commissioned, §2), worn by Shen for years, used at the bridge with the code exactly as established in §2 (一长两短 = "chase this way"), dropped/left behind, retrieved by Gu, carried by him ever after (§1, §5). No contradiction.
- **Asteria (白芷) motif**: self-planted on the exile slope (§1/§5) and echoed by the clinic ruins' surviving asteria (§5) — consistent and well-used.
- **Numbers**: ~100 immovable injured, ~20 extra stretchers, "3 days → needs 4.5, tampering buys 0.5, gap of 1 day" — internally consistent arithmetic.
- **Timeline math** (see above): order's "三日之内" vs. the ~10-day actual unfolding is not reconciled — **Must Fix**.
- **"没提一句沈砚"**: stated as an absolute ("didn't mention Shen at all"), then Gu immediately answers a direct question about Shen by name — not a hard contradiction (he didn't *volunteer* it) but the wording overclaims; worth tightening.
- **Wind risk payoff**: §3 sets up the risk conditionally on a north wind ("若是那晚起了北风"); §4 pays it off only as "风比预想的大" (stronger, not specified as northerly) — an implicit rather than explicit callback.

## Must Fix

1. **Timeline inconsistency**: the order's "三日之内" deadline for burning the granary/demolishing the bridge is never reconciled with the ~10-day span the text actually depicts (day 4 discovery → day 7 confrontation → day 10 action), and Zheng Han's established meticulousness makes the unremarked week-long overrun implausible.
2. **POV contamination**: the in-clinic passage in §4 ("苏晚已经在药庐里指挥了…") is narrated as direct fact from inside a room Gu never entered, breaking the story's own pattern of routing Su Wan's interiority through his direct observation. Needs a hedge, a witness, or removal of the unmediated interiority.
3. **Evidentiary contradiction**: the confession scene's stated standard ("看批文和粮册上是谁的笔迹、谁的核算") is in tension with Gu claiming sole authorship of tampering that §3 shows was physically done by Shen's hand — the story sets up a evidentiary rule it then doesn't honor at the one moment it matters most.

## Should Fix

1. Reduce the pivot to a single point of bad luck rather than two (wind + reassigned patrol) — causally link the second patrol's dispatch to the fire itself.
2. Tighten "没提一句沈砚" so it doesn't read as a literal claim contradicted two lines later.
3. Clarify the transition in §3 between "收起册子,往粮草库去" and "三日后" — same visit or three days apart is currently ambiguous.
4. Make the wind-risk payoff explicit (north wind, stronger than feared) rather than only "风比预想的大," so the planted Chekhov's gun visibly fires as planted.

## Optional

1. The whistle's chip/notch ("哨口边缘一点缺口") is introduced as a mystery in §1 and never explained — either seed its origin or accept it as pure atmosphere.
2. Consider cutting or softening the closing thesis-statement sentences ("他年轻时以为,守口如瓶是怯懦的另一个名字…") — the title and imagery already carry the theme; spelling it out this directly reads as over-explanation.
3. Three separate "flash-forward" narrator intrusions pre-announce doom (§2 twice, §3 once) — trimming to one would preserve more suspense for the climax.
