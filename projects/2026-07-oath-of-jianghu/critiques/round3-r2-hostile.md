# R2 Hostile Craft Critic — 《三缄》Round 3

## Hostile read notes

This is a competent draft with real architecture — the frame narrative (old man, hidden scar, white
angelica) pays off cleanly, the oath/whistle-code/triage-ethic setups all fire in the climax, and
the two deaths are each rooted in an established character trait rather than parachuted in. That's
more than most drafts manage. But on a hostile pass, the climax's evidentiary machinery does not
survive scrutiny, and the ending tips from "restrained" into "captioned."

**(a) Is the fire caused by choice or luck?**
Better than a pure-luck draft, but not clean. Three days before the operation, Gu Changfeng and
Shen Yan explicitly flag the woodpile at the city-wall base as a risk and assign Zhang Laoshuan — a
named character with an established skill ("专司看更、辨风向") — to watch it. At the moment of
crisis, Gu Changfeng *himself* pulls Zhang Laoshuan off that post to cover an unexpected checkpoint,
consciously weighing the risk ("两害相权") and leaving only "多盯着点风向" behind. That is real
agency creating the vulnerability. But the actual ignition still needs the wind to be "比预想的大" —
an unmotivated act of nature is what turns his choice into a corpse. The story wants it both ways:
Gu Changfeng owns the decision, but nature pulls the trigger. This is defensible tragic causation,
not "an extra sneezed and started a fire," but a hostile reader will notice the wind is still doing
load-bearing work at the exact moment it's needed, and the text doesn't dramatize Gu Changfeng
recognizing the rising wind and gambling anyway — it just reports the wind exceeded expectation.
Tighten this so the choice, not the weather, is unmistakably the last domino.

**(b) Does the confession's handwriting logic hold?**
No — this is the sharpest hole in the draft. Section 三 establishes: (1) on Day 4, 郑瀚 personally
shows Gu Changfeng a specific tampered batch ("这一批,写的是三日内运完...有人在做手脚") — this
predates the handover, so by the story's own later logic ("沈砚这几日的笔迹就此打住") it must carry
**Shen Yan's** handwriting; (2) three days later, at the confrontation, Gu Changfeng takes over all
future document-writing so that "沈砚这几日的笔迹就此打住,不再往批文上添一个字." In the trial
scene (Section 五), the narration claims the only documents surfaced are the post-handover ones in
Gu Changfeng's hand, while "更早几日沈砚亲笔核算的那一本,始终没有被人从底下翻出来." But the
Day-4 batch was never "buried" — 郑瀚 already had it in hand and pointed at it directly. It cannot
simultaneously be the trigger for the whole investigation and a document that was "never dug up."
The text never shows this specific document being destroyed, corrected, or reconciled. As written,
the resolution's central mechanism — why Gu Changfeng's confession can shield Shen Yan at all — has
a hole a hostile reader falls straight through.

**(c) Is the timeline consistent?**
Yes, this part holds up. Order issued Day 0 ("十日之内"); Day 4, 郑瀚 flags tampering and assigns
investigation; "三日后" (Day 7) confrontation and handover; "三日后夜里" (Day 10) the operation,
which lands exactly on the order's ten-day deadline — and cleverly double-uses the mandated trestle
demolition as the trap. Shen Yan's line "离限期只剩三日" at Day 7 checks out arithmetically. The one
soft spot is the ledger math itself ("剩下的三日改出来也是四日半,还是不够") — the conversion from
"3 days left" to "4.5 days of transport capacity" is not clearly explained and will cost a careful
reader a re-read, but it isn't a contradiction, just underspecified.

**Other tells.** The ending states its own theme almost verbatim: "三缄其口这四个字,到老了才落到
他自己身上,不再是书里的话" and "他年轻时以为,守口如瓶是怯懦的另一个名字" — this is a caption,
not an investigation; the title has already done this work more quietly through action (the whistle
never blown, the "当年犯了事" refrain), and stating it this bluntly is the kind of thing the rubric's
Originality anchor explicitly flags. The opening/closing "没写完的信" / "没有寄出的信" bookend is a
nice device but is mirrored almost too exactly — it reads slightly engineered rather than discovered.
And the "却" contrastive pivot ("却谁也没破过," "却没有辩," "却终究没有说全," "却也不是谎言," "却
没有一个字提到通敌"...) recurs so often across every major beat that by Section 四–五 it becomes a
predictable rhythm rather than a voice.

## Must Fix
1. **Close the handwriting hole.** The Day-4 batch that 郑瀚 personally showed Gu Changfeng ("有人在做手脚") predates the handover and must be in Shen Yan's hand, yet the trial scene claims earlier documents were "从未被翻出来." Either show this specific document being neutralized before trial, or have 郑瀚's silence about it be an explicit, acknowledged choice on his part — right now it's an unaddressed contradiction, not an ambiguity.
2. **Make the fire's ignition unmistakably choice-driven, not weather-driven.** Currently Gu Changfeng's reassignment of Zhang Laoshuan creates the vulnerability, but "the wind was stronger than expected" is what actually kills people. Give him a moment of recognizing the risk rising in real time and choosing anyway, so nature isn't doing the last, decisive push alone.

## Should Fix
1. Cut or bury the explicit theme statements ("三缄其口这四个字,到老了才落到他自己身上..." / "他年轻时以为,守口如瓶是怯懦的另一个名字") — the title and the "当年犯了事" refrain already carry this; stating it flattens it.
2. Vary the "却"-pivot construction — it recurs at nearly every major turn (Sections 二 through 五) and becomes a tic rather than a voice by the climax.
3. Clarify the "三日改出来也是四日半" math in the ledger confrontation — as written it requires the reader to reverse-engineer what's being compared.
4. Give Shen Yan one concrete beat that isn't in service of the plan or the oath — right now his interiority is almost entirely instrumental to the reader's understanding of the scheme.

## Optional
1. The "没写完的信" / "没有寄出的信" opening-closing mirror is a little too exact; consider letting the closing image rhyme rather than restate.
2. 郑瀚's mercy (writing "流放" instead of "斩") is thematically apt but mechanically reads as a legal technicality; one more beat of his interiority at the pen-hover moment would make it feel like a choice rather than a loophole.
