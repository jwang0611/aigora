# R3 Structural Analyst — 《三缄》Round 3

## Causal chain map

**§1 暮年 (frame)** — Old man, scarred face, unspoken whistle, unmailed-letter imagery. Pure setup/mystery; legitimate frame, not a dead scene (pays off at the end).

**§2 义结** — Establishes: (a) the Gu/Shen/Su triangle and the private pact; (b) the bronze whistle and its two-tone code (chase-signal vs. stay-back signal); (c) Zheng Han's scorched-earth order with a 10-day deadline and a "document tampering = treason = death" decree; (d) Shen Yan (as grain quartermaster, sole custodian of the evacuation math) discovers ~100 immovable wounded can't be evacuated in time and starts altering documents; (e) Su Wan's inflexible triage rule (injury-severity, not connections) — a clean plant for §4. All causal, nothing decorative.

**§3 粮册** — Day 4: Zheng Han catches a batch discrepancy, tasks Gu (his guard captain) with investigating. Day 7: Gu traces it to Shen Yan, they recalculate together and find the forgery alone buys only half a day against a 1.5-day shortfall — a genuine *decision turn*: Gu proposes 调虎离山 (decoy fire to pull the search party off the evacuation street). They explicitly identify the wall-base woodpile as a wind-risk (one narrow alley from the clinic street) and assign Zhang Laoshuan as a safety valve (watch wind, douse, or whistle-warn). Gu takes over all future document-writing himself, ending Shen Yan's paper trail from this point forward. Action set for day 10 (deadline night) — internally consistent with Shen Yan's "只剩三日" line at day 7.

**§4 青峡雪 (climax)** — Fire lights on schedule; decoy partially works. Complication: the search party, not fully convinced, posts a second checkpoint threatening to unravel the decoy. Gu has only one spare hand — Zhang Laoshuan — and makes the costly, foreshadowed choice to pull him off the woodpile to cover the checkpoint instead. *This is the load-bearing turn the task asked about, and it is earned*: it's a direct payoff of the risk planted in §3, forced by competing pressures, not luck. What follows — a stronger-than-expected north wind pushing the fire onto the now-unwatched woodpile, then through the alley to the clinic — is an environmental factor, but it was explicitly pre-seeded as a known risk in §3 ("这几日雪一直不大不小,火头一直没起" / "这堆柴不能留着不管"), so the wind is the trigger, Gu's choice is the loaded gun. This reads as "of course," not "really?" — good craft, not a coincidence violation. Gu then makes a second costly decision turn (commit all hands to the clinic street, can't split to help Shen Yan). Su Wan dies enacting her §2-established triage rule. Shen Yan, cornered, correctly uses the §2-established whistle code to lure pursuers onto the bridge and detonates it, dying with them; his cut-off "其实我一直——" pays off the earlier-planted ambiguity about his feelings for Su Wan (§2's "这只手比他师父的手稳" beat).

**§5 三缄** — Trial. Gu confesses everything material but shapes the wording to shield Shen Yan (technically true, materially incomplete — a legitimate, well-executed omission, not a knowledge violation since the narration flags it as such: "这句供词...终究没有说全"). Zheng Han's established character trait (judges only on submitted evidence, never on presumed intent — planted in §2, restated in §5) is what lets him choose exile over execution; this is earned characterization, not a loophole invented on the spot. Frame closes on the whistle/white-angelica bookend.

No dead scenes found. Every named prop (whistle, code, woodpile, triage rule, Zheng Han's evidentiary temperament) is planted and paid off.

## Knowledge-state audit

- **§4 clinic interior** ("苏晚仍按旧例排序...自己回身去背这最后两个"): Gu never enters the clinic ("没能进药庐一步"). The text explicitly frames this as reconstructed testimony — "是后来从抬出来的伤员、还有一个逃出来的药童口中，一点一点拼出来的" — before narrating it with novelistic certainty. This is the *legitimate* "off-page/told" channel the KnowledgeAuditor allows (documentary-reconstruction convention), not POV contamination. No violation.

- **Whistle code usage** (§4, bridge): Shen Yan blows "一声长的、两声短的," matching the §2-established code ("一长两短是'往这边追'") exactly. Correct payoff, no drift — though the narrator's gloss "往这边追,别往别处去" adds words not in the original code definition (minor, cosmetic).

- **POV discipline — flag**: The story is otherwise a consistent limited-third on Gu Changfeng (young and old), including the frame. But in §5, the sentencing paragraph moves fully inside Zheng Han's legal reasoning — "此刻摆在郑瀚面前的，恰恰只是一份未经军令、擅自行事的供词，够不够'通敌'两个字，落在他这支笔上，终究还有一线可斟酌的地方" — and then explicitly notes "没有人看出他这一瞬停了多久,也没有人问," which all but confirms Gu has no access to this. This is a genuine head-hop out of the established POV character into an antagonist's/judge's interiority at the story's most consequential decision. It doesn't break causal logic (the reader isn't misled about facts), but it is a POV-contract break at a moment the story otherwise controls tightly. Flag as **Should Fix**: either reframe as inference/rumor Gu later forms, or commit to an omniscient-narrator contract explicitly (the frame narration in §1/§5's historical aside could support this reading, but it needs to be consistent, not switched on only here).

## Consistency check

- **Military deadline vs. days elapsed**: Order given day 0. "密令下达第四日" Zheng Han flags discrepancy (day 4) → "三日后" Gu confronts Shen Yan (day 7), where Shen Yan says "离限期只剩三日" (deadline = day 10) → "三日后夜里" action is set (day 10 night) → trial "天亮后" (day 11 morning). This is internally consistent — the action happens on the deadline's own final night, which also raises the stakes appropriately. No timeline error here.

- **Handwriting claim in §5 — Must Fix**: The resolution hinges on "调虎离山前后最后改动的那几本，正是顾长风自己接手誊写用印的那一段，笔迹确凿，赖不掉...更早几日沈砚亲笔核算的那一本,始终没有被人从底下翻出来" (the early batches in Shen Yan's own hand were never dug up). But §3 opens with Zheng Han *already* holding "几份撤离批文" with discrepancies on **day 4** — three days *before* Gu takes over the writing (which only happens after the day-7 confrontation, per "这几日剩下的批文,我来写"). Since Shen Yan is established as the sole person handling this calculation at that point (§2: "撤离的人数、车马、路程，都要经他手核算"), those day-4 flagged batches logically bear his handwriting — and Zheng Han's team already possesses them; that's literally what triggers the whole investigation. The claim that Shen Yan's handwritten evidence "was never dug up" directly contradicts this. Nothing in the text shows those original day-4 documents being destroyed, replaced, or otherwise removed from evidence — the mechanism that would make the ending's central legal/moral claim true is never shown or even referenced. This is a continuity violation at the load-bearing climax of the story (Gu's entire final sacrifice/cover story rests on this claim), and per the blind-review protocol, is Must Fix regardless of scores.

- **Single whistle vs. two-way code** (§2): "两人还打了一枚铜哨" (a single whistle, forged once) is then used to establish a bidirectional signal code ("一长两短...一短两长") as if either party could send either signal — but only one whistle exists, and it ends up permanently on Shen Yan's person from youth through the climax. Minor plausibility wrinkle, not a hard contradiction (plausible reading: whoever carries the whistle on a given outing uses the code), worth a one-line clarification. Optional.

- Numbers (~100 wounded, ~20 extra stretchers, 1.5-day shortfall math) check out consistently across sections.

## Must Fix

1. **Handwriting-evidence contradiction (§3 vs §5)**: §3 establishes that Zheng Han already possesses suspicious evacuation batches on day 4, before Gu takes over document-writing — these must logically be in Shen Yan's own hand. §5 asserts Shen Yan's handwritten evidence was "never dug up." This is an unaddressed continuity break at the story's central moral/legal hinge (why Gu alone bears the guilt). Fix by either: (a) adding a beat showing Gu deliberately recovering/re-copying/destroying the day-4 batches after the day-7 confrontation, or (b) establishing that the day-4 flagged documents were clerk-transcribed fair copies (obscuring Shen Yan's original handwriting) distinct from the working ledger Gu confronts him with in §3, stated explicitly rather than left to inference.

## Should Fix

1. **POV break in §5 sentencing paragraph**: Narration moves fully inside Zheng Han's legal reasoning ("够不够'通敌'两个字，落在他这支笔上...没有人看出他这一瞬停了多久"), breaking the otherwise consistent Gu-centered limited POV at the story's most consequential moment. Either reframe as something Gu infers/hears about later, or commit explicitly to an omniscient-narrator contract consistent with the frame sections.

## Optional

1. Whistle-code phrasing in §4 ("往这边追,别往别处去") slightly expands the original §2 code definition ("往这边追") — tighten to match verbatim, or note it as Gu's later interpretive gloss.
2. Single-whistle-for-two-way-code setup (§2) is a minor plausibility wrinkle; one clarifying clause (e.g., who carries it day to day) would remove reader friction.
3. The math exposition in §3 ("剩下的三日改出来也是四日半,还是不够...沈砚的改法,顶多是往后拖了半日") is dense enough that a reader may need a second pass; consider a slightly more explicit restatement of what Shen Yan's forgery bought vs. what was still needed.
