No dead scenes found — every scene sets up a later payoff (triage rule → Su Wan's death;
Zheng Han's evidence-only creed → trial outcome; whistle code → bridge signal; woodpile
risk conversation → conflagration). The one place leaning on unearned convenience is the

POV audit: Narration is anchored to Gu Changfeng from §2–§4, framed by a more distant/
omniscient voice in §1 and §5 (a deliberate frame-narrative device, not a violation). The
reconstruction of Su Wan's death is correctly sourced as secondhand testimony. Two soft

## Consistency check

**Handwriting claim at trial — CONSISTENT.** §3 establishes that Gu Changfeng personally
reclaims and rewrites *every* tampered batch, including the one Zheng Han himself
flagged, and that from that point forward Shen Yan's hand never touches another altered
document. §5's claim that the surviving evidence is entirely in Gu's hand is accurate: it
wasn't hidden, it was destroyed. This is a properly fired Chekhov's gun, not a patched-over
hole. No fix needed here.

**Military deadline vs. elapsed days — CONSISTENT.** Order given Day 0 (10-day deadline).
Discrepancy flagged Day 4. Confrontation Day 7 ("离限期只剩三日" checks out: 10−7=3).
Action set for Day 10 night, exactly at the deadline. No contradiction found.

**One residual soft spot (Should Fix):** the pre-planted bridge demolition charge ("预先埋
