# R1 Naive Reader — 《三缄》Round 3

## Read-through notes

**第一节 (暮年)**: Hooked immediately — the branded old man, the whistle he won't blow, the white flowers "like an unfinished letter." I don't know who he is or what happened, but I want to. The line "他要说的话，早在三十年前那个雪夜，就已经用完了" told me exactly what kind of story this is going to be: a story about a man who ran out of words. Good promise made on page one.

**第二节 (义结)**: Character intro is clean — I could track Gu Changfeng, Shen Yan, Su Wan without confusion, and the sworn-brotherhood pact ("谁都不许先惊动苏晚的心") gave me a clear emotional stake early. Then I hit a paragraph that made me stop and reread: "顾长风记得这十几年里唯一一次险些破例…苏晚给他上药…他疼得直冒汗…他才低声跟顾长风说，这只手比他师父的手稳。顾长风当时笑他没出息，笑完自己站在廊下，也看了那双手很久——这话他没说给任何人听。" On first pass I attributed the "watching her hands for a long time, never told anyone" to Shen Yan, since he was the subject of the whole preceding clause. It's only on a second read that I realized the subject silently shifted to Gu Changfeng himself — meaning *he* also has buried feelings for Su Wan. That's a good reveal, but I nearly missed it, and it matters later. The evacuation order and Su Wan's triage rule ("伤重的先看，不是官大的先走") landed well — I believed her, and I liked her immediately without being told to.

**第三节 (粮册)**: The investigation-and-confrontation scene worked — I liked that Gu Changfeng doesn't ask "why" first, he just re-does the math, colder and more competent than emotional. The 调虎离山 plan is laid out clearly enough that I could follow it, though the logistics (车马数, 批文, 栈道, 哨卡) piled up fast — I was tracking mechanics more than feeling anything for a page or two. The one line that snagged me productively: Shen Yan's "这些年，有些话我一直没跟你说" — then he swallows it. I filed that away, waiting for it to pay off.

**第四节 (青峡雪)**: This is where the story really grabbed me. The wind shift felt earned, not convenient — the woodpile risk was flagged three days earlier, and it's a *decision* (reassigning Zhang Laoshuan) that causes the disaster, not blind luck. I felt the dread build in real time as Gu Changfeng smells smoke from the wrong direction. Su Wan's death is the strongest moment in the story — "她背上的老兵还有气息，她自己没有了。她的手还搭在其中一人的肩上，没有松开" — no adjectives doing the crying for me, and I felt it anyway. Gu Changfeng wiping the ash off her face — "这个动作他活了三十多年，从没在她面前做过" — landed hard, precisely because of the earlier corridor scene I'd almost missed. Then the bridge: Shen Yan's unfinished "其实我一直——" cut off by the explosion. I wanted that sentence. I still want it. The story tells me Gu Changfeng spent years guessing and never found out — which is thematically honest, but as a reader I felt the loss of that sentence almost as much as the loss of the man.

**第五节 (三缄)**: The trial scene is quietly devastating — Gu Changfeng's confession is true in every clause and still not the whole truth, and I believed both halves of that at once. Zheng Han's sentence (exile + branding instead of execution) felt consistent with the "赏罚只认凭据" characterization planted in section two — not a rescue, just the letter of a cold code working out slightly in his favor. The closing return to the white flowers and "终究没有寄出的信" closed the loop from page one exactly right. The last stretch ("又过了几年，北虏那一支终究没能再南下…") moves in summary rather than scene — I felt the story downshift from lived moments to stated conclusions right when I wanted one more beat of feeling, not exposition.

A few sentences throughout state the theme more than dramatize it — "这一瞬的取舍并不比战场上的任何一次取舍更难做，难的是做完之后要记一辈子" and "少年那句私约管住了他这些年的嘴，这一次，管住的是别的东西" read like the narrator explaining the point to me rather than trusting me to get there. I noticed these because everywhere else the story lets the image do the work (the whistle, the ash, the flowers) — these lines broke that pattern.

## Should Fix
